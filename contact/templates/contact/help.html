ViroCon helps you to design marine structures which need to withstand load combinations based on wave, wind and current. It lets you identify extreme environmental conditions with a defined return period using the environmental contour method.

Two specific environmental contour methods are available: The Highest Density Contour (HDC) method [1] and the Inverse First Order Reliability Method (IFORM) [2]. The environment can be described with a probability density function based on the so-called Conditional Modeling Approach [3]. ViroCon's methods align with current recommended practices dealing with environmental conditions and environmental loads [3].

The arrows at the home site guide you through the work process of deriving an environmental contour. Measurement files and probalistic models can be saved online and are associated with your profile.
