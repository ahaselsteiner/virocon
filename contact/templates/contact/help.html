The main task of this website is to compute an environmental contour. An environmental contour lets you define load cases based on wave, wind and current in a rational way.

Two specific environmental contour methods are available, the Highest Density Contour (HDC) method [1] and the Inverse First Order Reliability Method (IFORM) [2]. An environmental contour is defined based on a probabilistic model and a return period. ViroCon's methods are chosen to align with current recommended practices dealing with environmental conditions and environmental loads [3].

You can either define a probabilistic model directly or fit a probabilistic model to measured data. The arrows at the home site guide you through the work process of deriving an environmental contour. Measurement files and probalistic models can be saved online and are associated with your profile.
