Probabilistic model

In ViroCon's context a probabilistic model represents a probabilistic description of the environment. It describes the long-term statistics of the environment with a multivariate distribution.

Conditonal modeling approach

In conditonal modeling aproach [BitnerGregersen1991] one environmental variable has a marginal distribution and all other environmental variables have conditonal distributions.

Environmental contour

An environmental contour is the boundary of the mathematical region, which makes up the environmental states, which must be considered in a design. Along the contour discrete extreme environmental design conditions (EEDCs) can be selected for ultimate load calculations. Environmental contours can be derived based on a probabilist model. There exist different mathematical definitions for for environmental contours.

ViroCon supports the following two definitons for environmental contours:

  • Inverse first order reliability method (IFORM) contour
  • Highest density contour (HDC)

Other definitions, which are currently not supported are:

  • Huseby's approach based on hyperplanes and the Monte Carlo method[Huseby2013]
  • Inverse second order reliability method (ISORM) contour [Chai2018]

Inverse first order reliability method (IFORM)

Contours based on the inverse first order reliability method (IFORM) are defined by hyperplanes in the standard normal space. Consequently, the probabilistic model is first transformed to the normal space. There the environmental contour is defined as a hypersphere. Then, this hypersphere is transformed back to the original space.
