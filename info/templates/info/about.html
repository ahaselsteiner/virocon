About

ViroCon helps you design marine structures, which need to withstand load combinations based on wave, wind and current. It lets you identify extreme environmental conditions with a defined return period using the environmental contour method.

Two specific environmental contour methods are available: The highest density contour (HDC) method [1] and the inverse first order reliability method (IFORM) [2]. The environment can be described with a probability density function based on the conditional modeling approach (CMA) [3]. ViroCon's methods align with current recommended practices dealing with environmental conditions and environmental loads [3].

Open. ViroCon is an open-source software under the permissive MIT license. Check out our GitHub repository .

Cite. If you are using ViroCon in your academic work, please cite ViroCon as: A.F. Haselsteiner, J. Lehmkuhl, T. Pape, K.-L. Windmeier, K.-D. Thoben (2018): ViroCon: A software to compute multivariate extremes for structural integrity calculations.
